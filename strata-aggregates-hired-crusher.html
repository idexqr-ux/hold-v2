Hired Crusher

Governed visibility for temporary crushing plant, hire responsibility, site setup and off-hire readiness.

Temporary hire

Language support

Approved workplace-language guidance helps drivers, contractors and site teams understand key instructions at the point of scan - especially around vehicle movement, fuel handling, plant activity, stockyard operations, weighbridge activity, environmental controls and emergency response.

Safety note: Safety-critical translations should be controlled and approved before use.

Hire Status / Ownership

Hire active

Temporary plant should remain distinct from owned assets, with hire terms and responsibility visible.

Authorised Use

Only authorised operators should start, stop, feed, clear or adjust the hired crusher.

Delivery / Setup Requirements

Setup check

Confirm delivery condition, level ground, guarding, exclusion zone, power or fuel needs and traffic routes.

Inspection & Service Responsibility

Daily checks, defect records and service ownership must be clear between site team, hire company and contractor.

Fuel / Refuelling Awareness if Diesel

If diesel powered, refuelling must align with spill controls, authorised storage and plant shutdown conditions.

Environmental Controls

Dust, noise, vibration, runoff, spill risk and temporary stock movements should remain visible during hire use.

Breakdown / Maintenance Escalation

Escalate early

Breakdowns, jams, abnormal vibration or damage should be routed before production pressure hides ownership risk.

Return / Off-Hire Checks

Clean-down, damage, hours, fuel state, attachments and documentation should be confirmed before off-hire.

Governance Visibility

Hold keeps temporary plant decisions visible alongside production, stockyard, environmental and audit records.
