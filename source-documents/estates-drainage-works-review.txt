ESTATES DRAINAGE WORKS REVIEW
North Campus Service Road and Courtyard Area
Executive Summary
This review considers whether the paused drainage improvement works on the North Campus Service Road and adjacent courtyard area should progress into the next phase of delivery.
The current recommendation is to proceed with revised drainage works following completion of additional contractor quotations, updated budget confirmation and agreement of operational access arrangements.
A final decision is expected during Quarter 3.
Background
Following periods of prolonged rainfall during the previous winter season, several areas of standing water were identified on the North Campus Service Road and adjacent pedestrian routes.
Multiple reports were received from Estates staff, occupiers and contractors regarding surface water accumulation, restricted access and concerns relating to slip hazards.
An initial investigation was commissioned by Estates Operations and identified a combination of drainage channel deterioration, partial blockage and inadequate water flow during periods of heavy rainfall.
Temporary mitigation measures have remained in place since February.
Operational Context
The affected area serves multiple operational functions including:
deliveries
contractor access
waste collection
maintenance vehicle movements
pedestrian access between buildings
Although the area remains operational, temporary barriers and diversion routes have increased journey times and reduced operational flexibility.
Several planned maintenance activities have required additional coordination as a result of restricted access arrangements.
Current operational impacts are considered manageable but undesirable over the longer term.
Health and Safety Considerations
Health and Safety observations have identified several concerns associated with the current condition.
Recorded observations include:
standing water following heavy rainfall events
increased slip potential on pedestrian routes
reduced visibility of uneven surfaces
temporary diversion routes crossing vehicle movement areas
No reportable incidents have occurred.
However, three minor slip-related near miss reports have been recorded during the review period.
The Health and Safety team has advised that long-term reliance on temporary controls is not considered desirable.
Engineering Assessment
Engineering inspection identified the following:
deterioration of existing drainage channels
localised subsidence adjacent to drainage runs
restricted flow capacity during peak rainfall
evidence of root ingress within sections of pipework
The engineering recommendation is replacement of the most heavily affected drainage sections together with local resurfacing works.
The assessment concludes that continued patch repairs are unlikely to provide a long-term solution.
Financial Overview
Current financial estimates indicate:
drainage replacement works: £42,500
resurfacing works: £18,000
contingency allowance: £8,000
Estimated total project value:
£68,500 excluding VAT
Funding provision exists within the Estates Capital Maintenance Programme.
However, final budget confirmation remains outstanding pending completion of procurement activity.
Procurement Considerations
Two contractor quotations have been received.
A third quotation remains outstanding.
Procurement has advised that additional market testing may strengthen value-for-money assurance before contract award.
Lead times for commencement of works are currently estimated at six to eight weeks following approval.
Governance Considerations
The principal governance question is whether sufficient evidence exists to support progression from temporary mitigation measures into full project delivery.
Evidence currently includes:
engineering inspection reports
operational observations
Health and Safety observations
contractor quotations
budget estimates
Further budget confirmation and procurement completion are required before final approval.
Risks
Risks associated with delaying the works include:
continued deterioration of drainage infrastructure
increased maintenance costs
growing reliance on temporary controls
operational disruption during periods of heavy rainfall
increased Health and Safety exposure
Risks associated with proceeding include:
project cost escalation
operational disruption during construction works
contractor availability constraints
Recommendation
The current recommendation is to proceed with revised drainage improvement works following completion of procurement activity and confirmation of final funding arrangements.
A final approval decision is expected during Quarter 3.
