Deployment Standards

Estate Tag Requirements

Terminology Note
Within estate operations, QR deployment points may be referred to as Tags. Hold recognises both terms interchangeably where aligned with local operational language.
  1. Use weather-resistant labels in exposed outdoor estate areas
  2. Position tags away from direct water ingress where possible
  3. Confirm readability during scheduled estate inspections
  4. Replace faded or damaged labels before operational guidance becomes unreliable
  5. Review restricted-access QR points during compliance audits
  6. Confirm operational links remain current after estate changes or contractor works

Deployment Guidance

Recommended QR Sizes

QR deployment size should reflect the operational setting, scan distance and physical conditions at the point of use.

  • Vehicle bay or loading area Recommended size: 140–150 mm · Typical scan distance: 2–4 metres
  • Forklift or equipment asset Recommended size: 60–80 mm · Typical scan distance: under 1 metre
  • Pedestrian guidance point Recommended size: 90–120 mm · Typical scan distance: 1–2 metres
  • Outdoor or exposed site point Recommended size: 120–150 mm · Use weather-resistant, anti-glare material

Security & Governance

Governed Operational QR Identity

Hold QR deployment points form part of a managed operational environment. QR replacements, reassignment and retirement actions are governed through operational review and audit workflows.

  • Operational ownership retained QR deployment linked to approved operational records
  • Replacement history maintained Retired and superseded QR references preserved for audit review
  • Unauthorised replacement monitoring Operational teams should report unrecognised or damaged QR tags immediately
  • Session-aware operational access Some operational guidance may be restricted outside approved environments

Operational Access

Session-Aware Guidance Access

Hold operational guidance can be configured to reduce unmanaged access persistence after staff leave approved operational environments or complete active shifts.

  • Operational session monitoring Some QR-linked guidance may require active operational context
  • Shift-aware access control Operational guidance can expire following shift completion
  • Offsite visibility management Selected operational records may be restricted outside approved environments
  • Revalidation support Operational users may be required to rescan approved QR points
  • Approved operational device recognition Selected operational guidance may be limited to recognised staff devices or approved operational sessions
