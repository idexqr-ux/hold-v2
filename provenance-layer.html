Hold / Provenance Layer

Provenance Through Context

The purpose of the Provenance Layer is to help users understand where information originally came from, how it entered the organisation, and what source record or evidence sits behind it.

Origin path

Core question

Where did this come from?

Trust shows where information came from, how current it is, and how it connects. Governance shows where responsibility sits. Provenance shows the origin path.

Contextual Identity

Known As

Flint

Purpose

Show how the asset, project, location or operational item may be referenced in conversations, emails, reports and historic records.

Why This Matters

Historic maintenance records, project documents and operational conversations may refer to this asset as Flint rather than Crusher CR-01.

Example User Questions

  • Why are people talking about Flint?
  • Is Flint the same as CR-01?
  • Why can't I find Flint in the asset register?

Contextual Identity helps users join operational conversations, emails, project records, maintenance history and asset registers without needing prior local knowledge.

Boundary

Provenance, Operational Memory and Contextual Identity should stay calm and lightweight. They show origin, reason and naming context without long document previews, evidence dumps or AI certainty language.

Connection to other layers

Companion concepts, scanned together.

Trust answers: Can I trust this?

Governance answers: Where does responsibility sit?

Provenance answers: Where did this come from?

Operational Memory answers: Why did this happen?

Provenance helps users understand where information came from. Operational Memory helps users understand why things changed. Contextual Identity helps users understand what something is known as. Together they help preserve the path back to understanding.
